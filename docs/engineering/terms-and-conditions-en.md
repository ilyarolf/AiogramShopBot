## 1. Payment Deadlines and Order Processing

### 1.1 Standard Payment Deadline
- After creating an order, you have **15 minutes** to complete the payment.
- The cryptocurrency amount to be paid is fixed at order creation and does not change.
- After the deadline expires, the order is automatically cancelled and reserved items are released.

### 1.2 Extended Payment Deadline for Partial Payments
- If you make a partial payment (less than the required amount), you receive **one-time** extension of **30 minutes**.
- The extension begins from the moment you are notified about the partial payment.
- You will receive a new payment address for the remaining amount.

### 1.3 Automatic Wallet Usage
- If you have a balance in your wallet, it will be automatically applied to your order.
- The invoice amount is reduced accordingly by your wallet balance.

## 2. Payment Validation and Tolerances

### 2.1 Exact Payment
- Payments within a tolerance of **0.1%** above the required amount are accepted as correct.
- The order is completed normally.

### 2.2 Minor Overpayment (≤0.1%)
- Overpayments up to and including **0.1%** of the required amount are forfeited in favor of the shop.
- No wallet credit is issued.
- **Example:** Order 0.00100000 BTC, you pay 0.00100050 BTC → 0.00000050 BTC is forfeited.

### 2.3 Overpayment (>0.1%)
- **No penalty fee**
- Payments exceeding **0.1%** above the required amount are automatically credited to your wallet.
- You will receive a notification about the credit.
- **Example:** Order €10.00, you pay €10.50 → €0.50 is credited to your wallet.

### 2.4 Underpayment - First Attempt
- **No penalty fee** (within extended deadline)
- If your first payment is insufficient, you receive one opportunity to pay the missing amount.
- The payment deadline is extended by **30 minutes**.
- You will receive a new payment address for the remaining amount.

### 2.5 Underpayment - Second Attempt
- **5% penalty fee**
- If your second payment is also insufficient, the order is automatically cancelled.
- All payments made so far are credited to your wallet, **minus 5% processing fee**.
- **Example:**
  - Required: €15.00
  - Paid: €12.00 + €2.00 = €14.00
  - Wallet credit: €14.00 - 5% = €13.30

### 2.6 Late Payment
- **5% penalty fee**
- Payments received after the payment deadline are automatically credited to your wallet, **minus 5% processing fee**.
- The order is cancelled and reserved items are released.
- **Goodwill provision:** If you can prove that your payment was sent on time (e.g., blockchain delay), the administrator may waive the penalty fee.

## 3. Exchange Rates and Cryptocurrencies

### 3.1 Exchange Rate Fixation
- The cryptocurrency amount is calculated based on the current exchange rate at order creation.
- **This amount is fixed** and does not change, even if the exchange rate fluctuates later.
- For wallet credits upon cancellation or partial payment, the original exchange rate is used.

### 3.2 Currency Validation
- You must use the cryptocurrency specified in the order.
- Payments in other cryptocurrencies are not accepted and cannot be assigned.

## 4. Wallet Balance

### 4.1 Usage
- Your wallet balance can **only be used for orders** in this shop.
- Withdrawal of the balance to an external cryptocurrency address is **not possible**.

### 4.2 Balance Sources
- **Top-Up:** Voluntary deposit via user menu
- **Overpayment:** Automatic credit for overpayment >0.1%
- **Cancellation:** Refund upon order cancellation (possibly minus penalty fee)
- **Late Payment:** Automatic credit (minus penalty fee)

### 4.3 Balance Forfeiture
- **Important:** The shop may cease operations at any time.
- In case of business closure, wallet balances are forfeited without compensation.
- There is no entitlement to withdrawal or refund of the balance.
- Wallet balances do not accrue interest or generate returns.

## 5. Penalty Fees - Justification

### 5.1 Why Penalty Fees?
The 5% penalty fee for late payments and multiple underpayments is justified by:

1. **Exchange Rate Risk:** The shop bears the risk of rate fluctuations between order creation and payment receipt.
2. **Transaction Costs:** Cryptocurrencies must be converted to fiat currency with associated fees.
3. **Opportunity Costs:** Reserved items cannot be sold to other customers.
4. **Administrative Effort:** Manual review and processing of payment issues.
5. **Blockchain Fees:** Potential refunds incur additional transaction costs.
